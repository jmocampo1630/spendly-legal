# Spendly Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2026

Spendly is a local-first budgeting and reminder app. This policy explains what data the app handles and where it goes.

## Data Stored On Your Device

Spendly stores your app data locally on your device, including:

- sessions, budgets, stores, items, categories, deductions, and cashback entries
- reminders for bills and expirations
- onboarding and app preference flags

This data is stored in the app's local SQLite database. Spendly does not run an account system or cloud sync in the current release.

## Gemini API Key

If you enable AI scanning, Spendly may store your Google Gemini API key on your device using encrypted secure storage. The key is used only to send scan requests from your device to Google's Gemini API.

You can remove the saved key from Settings at any time.

## AI Scanning And Image Uploads

When you scan a receipt, price tag, checklist, bill, or expiration item, Spendly sends the selected image and scan prompt to Google's Gemini API so the image can be interpreted.

Spendly uses the response to fill in details inside the app, such as item names, prices, stores, due dates, or reminder details.

Spendly does not store your scanned images on its own servers. In the current release, Spendly does not operate a backend server for AI scanning. Google's handling of submitted images is governed by Google's applicable terms and privacy policies.

## Notifications

Spendly can schedule local notifications for reminders you create. Notification schedules are stored on your device and are used to alert you before bills or expirations are due.

## Backups And Imports

If you export a backup, Spendly creates a JSON backup file containing your app data. You choose where to save or share that file. If you import a backup, the selected file is read by Spendly and used to replace the app's local data.

## Analytics, Ads, And Crash Reporting

The current release of Spendly does not include advertising, analytics tracking, or crash reporting. If crash reporting is added in a future release, this policy will be updated before that release ships.

## Data Deletion

You can delete app data by deleting sessions, reminders, categories where allowed, or by uninstalling the app. Uninstalling Spendly removes the local app database and locally stored settings from your device.

## Contact

For privacy questions, contact the app developer through the support contact listed on Spendly's Play Store page.
